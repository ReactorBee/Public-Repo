Privacy Policy

Last Updated: September 14, 2025

Liferay Fragment Builder Chrome Extension

Effective Date: July 04, 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how the Liferay Fragment Builder Chrome Extension ("Extension") handles your information. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting it.

Note: This Extension is not affiliated with or endorsed by Liferay, Inc. Liferay is a registered trademark of Liferay, Inc.

Google API Services: This Extension's use of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

1 Information We Process

The Extension processes the following information locally in your browser:

  • HTML/CSS Content: The Extension processes the HTML and CSS of web pages you interact with to extract the selected elements. This processing happens entirely in your browser.
  • Extension Settings: Your preferences and settings within the Extension are stored locally in your browser's storage.

Important: We do not collect, store, or transmit this information to any external servers.

2 How We Process Your Information

The Extension processes information to:

  • Extract and format HTML/CSS from selected page elements
  • Generate and download code snippets to your local machine
  • Remember your preferences within the Extension

All processing happens locally in your browser. No data is sent to our servers.

3 Data Storage

The Extension uses the following browser storage mechanisms:

  • chrome.storage.local: For storing your extension preferences
  • Browser Memory: For temporary processing of selected elements

All data remains on your local machine and is never transmitted to any external servers. Your extracted code is only saved if you explicitly choose to download it.

4 Permissions Used

The Extension requests the following permissions:

  • activeTab: To access the current tab's content when you activate the extension
  • scripting: To inject content scripts that help with element selection
  • downloads: To save extracted code to your computer
  • storage: To save your extension preferences locally

These permissions are standard for Chrome extensions and are necessary for the Extension's core functionality.

5 Data Security

Since all data processing happens locally in your browser:

  • Your data never leaves your computer unless you choose to download it
  • We don't have access to the web pages you visit or the elements you select
  • Your extension preferences are stored securely in your browser's local storage

For maximum security, always ensure your browser and operating system are up to date.

6 Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be indicated by the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes.

7 Your Control Over Data

Since all data is stored locally in your browser, you have full control over it:

  • View/Delete Data: You can view or delete the Extension's stored data through your browser's extension settings
  • Disable/Remove: You can disable or remove the Extension at any time through Chrome's extension management
  • Permissions: You can review and modify the Extension's permissions in Chrome's settings
